// ABOUT

A career built on reading rooms, not staying in one lane.

Sean Cusick is a Scottish consultant and educator based in Warsaw. Nineteen years across hospitality, international development, the space industry, data, and language — all drawing on the same underlying skill.

// THE PRACTICE
One diagnostic skill. Two doors in.

Sùil means eye and hope in Scottish Gaelic — rooted in an older word for the sun, as the eye of the sky. That double meaning is the thesis of this practice: see clearly first, then find the way forward.

The breadth in Sean's career isn't a detour. Running a 300-person NGO under pressure, advising a space-tech startup that needed €500k before it could operate, managing a hotel through political upheaval, teaching English to adult professionals who are navigating a second language and a second culture simultaneously — all of it is practice in reading what's actually going on in a room, and then helping the people in it move.

Suil Systems offers that skill in two directions: to organisations that need clearer communication, sharper strategy, or a way through conflict — and to individuals who want fluency, structure, or a space to think out loud with someone who will be direct with them.

// WHERE THIS IS GOING
The longer aim.

Something happens consistently in the coaching and English sessions: clients start talking about what's actually going on, not just the surface task they came with. Several have said, unprompted, that Sean should be doing this professionally — as a therapist or counsellor.

The plan is to take that seriously. Suil Systems generates the income and the structured client hours that fund and create space for formal training in counselling or psychotherapy. That credential is the medium-term goal — not a pivot away from the consulting and coaching work, but a deepening of it.

NOW
Executive coaching and consulting in Warsaw. Business English. Strategic communication. Building the anchor client base.
NEXT
Enrol in a part-time counselling or psychotherapy programme. Transition toward qualified therapeutic practice over 3–5 years, alongside the consulting work.
